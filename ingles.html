Past Continuous

English Grammar: Past Continuous

The Past Continuous tense describes actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

Structure

Subject + was/were + verb(-ing)

  • I was reading a book.
  • They were playing football.

Uses

  • To describe an action happening at a specific time in the past.
  • To show two actions happening at the same time.
  • To give background information in a story.

Examples

  • At 8 pm last night, I was watching TV.
  • While she was cooking, he was setting the table.
  • The sun was shining and the birds were singing.

Tips

  • Use was with singular subjects (I, he, she, it).
  • Use were with plural subjects (we, you, they).
  • Often combined with while or when to show simultaneous actions.

Future Simple

English Grammar: Future Simple (will)

The Future Simple tense is used to express actions that will happen in the future.

Structure

Subject + will + verb (base form)

  • I will study tomorrow.
  • They will travel next week.

Uses

  • To express decisions made at the moment of speaking.
  • To predict future events.
  • To promise or offer something.

Examples

  • I will help you with your homework.
  • The weather will be sunny tomorrow.
  • She will call you later.

Tips

  • will is the same for all subjects (I, you, he, she, we, they).
  • Use contractions in informal speech: I'll, you'll, they'll.
  • Combine with time expressions: tomorrow, next week, in 2026.
